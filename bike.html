Anglesey Abbey is a country house, formerly a priory, in the village of Lode, 5 ¹⁄₂ miles northeast of Cambridge, England. Visit the Jacobean-style house, the marvellous gardens and the working watermill.

Duxford airplane museum

Founded in 1977, the Imperial War Museum Duxford is Britain's largest aviation museum. Duxford houses the museum's large exhibits, including nearly 200 aircraft, military vehicles, artillery and minor naval vessels in seven main exhibition buildings



Wicken Fen

This nature reserve is the National Trust's oldest nature reserve, and England's most famous fen. Go for a short or a longer walk,take some fantastic shoots of rare bird species. Treat yourself to a cup of coffee and a tasty slice of homemade cake from the Docky Hut Café at the end of your walk.



St Yves

St Ives is a market town and civil parish in Cambridgeshire, England. St Ives lies about 5 miles east of Huntingdon and 12 miles north-west of the city of Cambridge. Simply follow the guided busway from Cambridge for a car free cycling trip. St Ives is situated within the non-metropolitan district of Huntingdonshire, which covers a similar area to the historic county of the same name.



Wimpole hall

Wimpole Estate is a large estate containing Wimpole Hall, a country house located within the Parish of Wimpole, Cambridgeshire, England, about 8 ¹⁄₂ miles southwest of Cambridge.
