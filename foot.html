Put your shoes on to discover Cambridge best outdoors areas and gardens



Cambridge Botanic Garden

Nested at the heart of the city, the botanic garden hosts many rare plants species. It has some fantastic greenhouse and a peaceful café. As a student of Cambrigde university you can enjoy for free the gardens all year round. Why not visiting them during their famous apple day in October or in the summer for one of their jazz nights?



Fen Ditton-The Plough

Follow the cam riverside to reach “The plough” in Fen Ditton. This gastropub is the perfect spot to watch the famous rowing bump race while sipping a cool beer or enjoying some fine British cuisine. They have gorgeous outside table all year round and the menu changes with every seasons.



Selwyn College Gardens

Established in the Victorian period, the beautiful College gardens have been developed into a landscape of extensive lawns, mature trees, richly planted flowering borders and beds, and collections of shrubs from around the world.



Granchester Orchard

This is an absolute must do in Cambridge surroundings! Walk, cycle or punt through Granchester Meadow to reach “The orchard tea garden”. This popular tea saloon opened in 1897 and has hosted many of the famous Cambridge students such as Newton, Darwin, Crick and Watson, Stephen Hawking, Alan Turing…Their fresh home-made scones are a simply delicious and will give you plenty of energy for your way back.



Emmanuel College and its ducks

Emmanuel was founded in 1584 on the site of a Dominican friary. Its gardens are some of the finest within any Cambridge college! Of Particular interest are the ponds, lawns and well stocked flower beds. The famous Emmanuel ducks wander throughout the grounds.
